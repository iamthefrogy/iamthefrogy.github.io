‚Üí The Power Questions
WHAT?
Define the problem clearly
Strip away the noise. What exactly is happening here? What are the facts vs. opinions? What am I actually being told?
"What evidence supports this claim?"
WHY?
Find the root cause ‚Äî dig deeper
Ask "why" five times in a row. Surface-level answers hide the real truth. Why does this matter? Why now? Why this way?
"Why do I believe this is true?"
HOW?
Understand the mechanism
How does this actually work? How was this conclusion reached? How reliable is the method? How can I verify this?
"How would I explain this to a 10-year-old?"
WHO?
Trace the source & motives
Who benefits from this? Who is saying this and why? Who is affected? Who is being left out of the conversation?
"Who has the most to gain or lose here?"
WHEN?
Context of time matters
When was this true? Is it still true? When did things change? Timing shapes relevance ‚Äî old data can mislead today's decisions.
"When was this last verified or updated?"
WHERE?
Context of place & scope
Where does this apply? Where does it break down? Is this universal or specific? A truth in one context can be a lie in another.
"Where else has this been tested?"
WHY NOT?
Invert the problem ‚Äî flip it
The most powerful question. Why NOT the opposite? What if the common answer is wrong? Inversion reveals blind spots others miss entirely.
"What if the opposite were true?"
WHAT IF?
Explore possibilities & consequences
Stretch your thinking. What if this fails? What if it succeeds beyond expectations? What are the second and third-order effects?
"What's the worst / best that can happen?"
‚Üí Thinking Frameworks To Apply Daily

üîç First Principles

  • Identify your assumption ‚Äî what do you "just know"?
  • Break it down to fundamental truths that cannot be reduced further
  • Question each piece ‚Äî is this actually true or just widely believed?
  • Rebuild your understanding from the ground up
  • Arrive at your OWN conclusion, not the borrowed one

üé≠ Steel Man Method

  • Find the STRONGEST version of the opposing argument
  • Argue FOR the other side as if it's your own position
  • Identify what's genuinely valid in that perspective
  • Only then build your counter ‚Äî now it's actually strong
  • If you can't steel man it, you don't truly understand it

üîÑ Second-Order Thinking

  • First-order: "What happens next?" (everyone does this)
  • Second-order: "And then what happens after that?"
  • Map the chain of consequences ‚Äî 2nd, 3rd, 4th order effects
  • Check: Who else will react? How will the system adapt?
  • The best decisions consider what most people overlook

‚ö° Biases That Hijack Your Thinking ‚Äî Watch Out!

Confirmation Bias
You seek info that proves what you already believe and ignore what doesn't.
Anchoring Effect
The first piece of info you get becomes the anchor for all your judgements.
Dunning-Kruger
The less you know, the more confident you feel. Expertise breeds humility.
Sunk Cost Fallacy
You keep going because you've invested, not because it still makes sense.
Bandwagon Effect
Everyone believes it, so it must be right. Popular ‚â† correct.
Availability Heuristic
What comes to mind easily feels more true or common than it actually is.
Survivorship Bias
You only see the winners. The graveyard of failures is invisible to you.
Authority Bias
Someone important said it, so it must be true. Titles don't equal truth.

‚ú¶ The 8 Non-Negotiable Rules ‚ú¶

01 Correlation is NOT causation. Just because two things happen together doesn't mean one caused the other.
02 Strong opinions, loosely held. Be willing to change your mind when evidence says so.
03 "I don't know" is the most powerful sentence. It's the beginning of learning, not the end.
04 Seek to understand before seeking to be understood. Listen first, judge later.
05 The map is not the territory. Your model of reality is always an approximation.
06 Check the incentives. When confused about why something is happening, follow the money.
07 Beware of false dichotomies. Most choices aren't either/or ‚Äî the truth often sits in between.
08 Think in probabilities, not certainties. Nothing is 100% ‚Äî assign confidence levels.
