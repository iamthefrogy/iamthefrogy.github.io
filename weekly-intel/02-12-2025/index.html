Additional Intelligence Sections

P1 | [NEW] Google Patches Two Actively Exploited Zero-Day Vulnerabilities in Android

  • What Happened: Google released its December 2025 monthly security update for the Android operating system, addressing 107 security vulnerabilities.
  • Why it Matters (BLUF): Two of the patched high-severity vulnerabilities, both affecting the Android Framework component, are confirmed to be under active exploitation in the wild. This presents an immediate and significant risk to all unpatched Android devices.
  • Facts:
  • On December 1, 2025, Google announced its monthly Android security patches, which included fixes for 107 distinct flaws (The Hacker News, 2025-12-02 07:17:00 UTC).
  • The flaws span multiple components including the Framework, System, Kernel, and hardware-specific components from Arm, MediaTek, and Qualcomm (The Hacker News, 2025-12-02 07:17:00 UTC).
  • Google's advisory explicitly stated that two of the patched vulnerabilities have been exploited in the wild (The Hacker News, 2025-12-02 07:17:00 UTC).
  • Assessment:
  • We assess with High Confidence that threat actors will rapidly attempt to reverse-engineer the patches to develop exploits for wider use. The confirmation of active exploitation means working exploits already exist, and patch analysis will lower the bar for other actors to replicate them.
  • We assess that the primary risk is to users who do not receive timely updates, either due to manufacturer delays or because their device is no longer supported. The fragmentation of the Android ecosystem exacerbates this risk.
  • The exploitation of Framework vulnerabilities often leads to privilege escalation, allowing malicious apps to gain system-level control, bypass security permissions, and access sensitive user data.
  • Relevance:
  • This threat is highly relevant due to the widespread use of Android devices for both corporate and personal use (BYOD).
  • Affected in estate? Unknown. An immediate inventory and patch status check of all managed Android devices is required.
  • ATT&CK:
  • T1452: Exploitation for Privilege Escalation
  • Detection Ideas:
  • On managed devices, monitor for unexpected application installations, changes in device configuration, or anomalous network traffic patterns that begin after a user visits a suspicious website or installs an app from an untrusted source.
  • Utilize Mobile Threat Defense (MTD) solutions to detect rooting, privilege escalation, and malicious application behavior.
  • Mitigations:
  • Apply the December 2025 Android security patches immediately.
  • Instruct users to only install applications from the official Google Play Store.
  • Ensure that Google Play Protect is enabled on all devices.

P1 | [NEW] 'ShadyPanda' Actor Compromises Browser Extensions with 4.3 Million Installations

  • What Happened: A threat actor campaign dubbed 'ShadyPanda' has been discovered compromising popular and legitimate browser extensions, turning them into spyware.
  • Why it Matters (BLUF): With over 4.3 million total installations, this campaign represents a significant supply chain risk, enabling widespread data theft and persistence on victim machines through a trusted software channel.
  • Facts:
  • The campaign has been active for at least seven years, with a surge in malicious changes introduced in mid-2024 (The Hacker News, 2025-12-01 17:29:00 UTC).
  • Five of the extensions began as legitimate programs before being updated with malicious code, accounting for 300,000 of the total installs (The Hacker News, 2025-12-01 17:29:00 UTC).
  • The compromised extensions were available for both Chrome and Edge browsers and have since been removed from the respective web stores (BleepingComputer, 2025-12-01 15:01:40 UTC).
  • Assessment:
  • We assess with High Confidence that this campaign is focused on espionage and credential theft. Browser extensions have privileged access to web content, including session cookies, form data, and user credentials, making them an ideal vector for this type of activity.
  • We assess that the threat of legitimate extensions being sold to or compromised by malicious actors is growing. This "bait-and-switch" tactic abuses the trust users place in established software with good reviews and a large install base.
  • The long-running nature of the campaign suggests the actor is patient and aims for long-term access rather than noisy, disruptive attacks.
  • Relevance:
  • This is highly relevant as browser extensions are widely used across the organization. Both Chrome and Edge are standard browsers.
  • Affected in estate? Unknown. An audit of installed browser extensions is necessary to identify potential exposure.
  • ATT&CK:
  • T1176: Browser Extensions
  • T1056.001: Keylogging
  • T1555.003: Credentials from Web Browsers
  • Detection Ideas:
  • Hunt for network traffic to suspicious domains originating from browser processes (e.g., chrome.exe, msedge.exe).
  • Use endpoint detection and response (EDR) tools to query for installed browser extensions and compare against known malicious extension IDs, if they become available.
  • Monitor for browser processes spawning unusual child processes, such as command shells or scripting interpreters.
  • Mitigations:
  • Implement an allow-list for browser extensions to restrict what users can install.
  • Conduct a mandatory audit of all currently installed browser extensions on corporate devices.
  • Raise user awareness about the risks of browser extensions, even those that appear legitimate, and advise them to grant extensions the minimum necessary permissions.

P2 | [UPDATE] CISA Adds Actively Exploited OpenPLC Vulnerability to KEV Catalog

  • What Happened: The U.S. CISA has added CVE-2021-26829, a cross-site scripting (XSS) vulnerability in OpenPLC ScadaBR, to its Known Exploited Vulnerabilities (KEV) catalog.
  • Why it Matters (BLUF): Inclusion in the KEV catalog confirms evidence of active, in-the-wild exploitation. This poses a significant risk to organizations using this open-source SCADA software in their operational technology (OT) environments.
  • Facts:
  • CISA added CVE-2021-26829 to the KEV catalog on or around November 28, 2025 (CISA, 2025-11-28 12:00:00 UTC).
  • The vulnerability is a cross-site scripting (XSS) flaw affecting both Windows and Linux versions of OpenPLC ScadaBR (The Hacker News, 2025-11-30 09:23:00 UTC; Security Affairs, 2025-12-01 08:59:15 UTC).
  • The vulnerability carries a CVSS v3.1 score of 5.4 (Security Affairs, 2025-12-01 08:59:15 UTC).
  • Assessment:
  • We assess with High Confidence that the risk is elevated despite the moderate CVSS score. Active exploitation in OT environments is a critical concern, as attackers can leverage XSS to steal operator session cookies, inject malicious scripts to manipulate the HMI, or pivot to other parts of the network.
  • While the initial vector is XSS, a successful exploit could serve as a foothold for more severe attacks against critical infrastructure.
  • Relevance:
  • This is relevant for any part of the organization that operates or manages OT or ICS environments.
  • Affected in estate? Unknown. A query must be sent to asset owners responsible for OT/ICS to determine if OpenPLC ScadaBR is in use.
  • ATT&CK (for ICS):
  • T0854: Spearphishing Attachment (as a potential delivery vector)
  • T0831: Manipulation of View
  • T0885: Establish Command and Control
  • Detection Ideas:
  • Monitor web server logs for OpenPLC ScadaBR for suspicious requests containing script tags or other XSS payloads.
  • Analyze network traffic for connections from the OT network to unusual external IP addresses, which could indicate a C2 channel established after a successful exploit.
  • Mitigations:
  • Federal Civilian Executive Branch (FCEB) agencies are required to remediate this vulnerability by the due date specified in the KEV catalog.
  • All organizations using OpenPLC ScadaBR should prioritize applying patches or mitigations provided by the vendor.
  • Ensure OT networks are properly segmented from corporate IT networks and not exposed to the public internet.
