Why These Tests Matter

These tests show if your browser is leaking potentially sensitive data. Fail means data was exposed (a potential privacy risk). Pass means the data was protected or inaccessible. “Skip” simply indicates the test couldn't run because the feature is not supported or something else prevented it.

Each test highlights how browsers can disclose information about your device and environment. Malicious actors or data-collecting entities can use these techniques for tracking, profiling, or even launching targeted attacks. For example:

  • Fingerprinting Attacks: Unique combinations of system details (like fonts, GPU, and screen resolution) can make you easy to track across different sessions and websites.
  • Geolocation Exploits: Revealing your physical location may pose safety or privacy risks, especially if combined with other personal data.
  • WebRTC Leaks: If your local IP addresses are exposed, attackers could try to bypass proxies, VPNs, or firewalls, or even attempt direct attacks on your network.

Remediation Tips: - Use privacy-focused browsers or plugins - Tighten permissions, disable unnecessary APIs - Use a reputable VPN or proxy to hide IP - Regularly clear cookies/storage - Be cautious about granting location or other intrusive permissions
